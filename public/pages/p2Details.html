Project 2 - CPSC 581

This was an interesting project to work on because the clients main focus was an interaction with a user that doesn't have a complete understanding of how computers acutally function and who cannot really interact with them in the traditional way. So I had to change my mindset to answer the question: How do I design for a toddler?



Learn alllll about it right here ↓

Promo Video



When I brainstormed interactions between grandparents and young children I found myself consistently falling back to the idea of peek-a-boo, hide and seek, and generally the idea of using an underdeveloped sense of object permanence to bring joy to a young child.
I thought it wouldn't be an immersive experience to just create a video call application and have the grandparents cover their own face with their hands because that felt superficial when they know they are behind a screen and not in person. I wanted the grandparents (Oma & Opa) to be able to have contorl over things that appear on the child's (Rhys) screen.
I believed that would make Oma and Opa feel like they were that much closer to their grandson Rhys.

And alongside having the ability to play virtual peek-a-boo, Oma & Opa can get Rhys's attention by virtually shaking one of his favourite toys. And once his attention is on them, they can snap a quick photo of him to document the visit!

Variations

Variation 1

Brainstorming a possible UI layouts.

Variation 2

Virtual hide-and-seek has Oma and Opa picking a location for their video feed to be stuck behind. Rhys has to find them.

Variation 3

Connects with the chaotic destruction inside Rhys! To see Oma and Opa again, Rhys must tear down the block wall obstructing his view of them.

Variation 4

Very simple Peek-a-Boo but with the ability to customize it with the appendages of Rhys's favourite animals.

Variation 5

Instead of an animation that moves the hands, Oma and Opa can move the hands around the screen with their mouse.

Variation 6

No video feed here for Rhys. Oma and Opa can see him however, while he is scrambling to get out of the view of the camera.

Variation 7

Peek-a-Boo but instead of a set of hands covering the screen, the audio visualization from a song being played covers Rhys's screen. Oma and Opa can choose which song it is.

Variation 8

Babies are so hard to get good pictures of. This one gets the baby to look right at the camera!

Variation 9

An easy way to let Rhys express himself vocally. Experimenting with different volume ranges to see what happens with the voice controlled hands.

Variation 10

Where's Waldo-esque vibes here. Let Rhys tap random objects in a virtual room and see the results applied to his own video feed.
